--- layout: post title: Image science --- A common belief is that temporary solutions last the longest. A simple bash script that used to handle my image resizing needs was an excellent example of such a solution. It was hard to find a good enough reason to write anything more sophisticated than a five liner in bash to resize a directory of images downloaded from my camera. Not until I started to use NeatImage for removing noise from my photos. It would be nice to be able to automatically rename and move files processed by NeatImage, and then resize them to a given percentage size, to finally upload them to my Flickr account. In a couple of hours I managed to write a simple script to do the resizing for me. It'd go faster if I'd known the OptionParser or the ImageScience classes beforehand :-). Nothing to regret though, works way faster than the bash & ImageMagick duo. ImageScience doesn't have ImageMagick's bells and whistles, however is perfect for image resizing in Ruby as it utilizes the fast FreeImage library written in C. What's left to do is to add some error handling, and implement additional options (e.g. sending to flickr). You can find my script on GitHub : http://github.com/lbadura/ruby-tidbits/tree/master/image_resizer/image_resizer.rb, feel free to fiddle around with it.
